About Stars

Stars are massive luminous spheres of plasma that light up the universe.
They are giant balls of heated gas, primarily composed of hydrogen and helium, make up stars.
In their cores, nuclear fusion produces energy, which is how they shine. The size, color, and brightness of stars can vary.
They form, exist for millions or billions of years, and then die, frequently leaving behind black holes, neutron stars, or white dwarfs. The nearest star to Earth is the Sun. They vary in size, brightness, and temperature, creating stunning displays in the night sky.
